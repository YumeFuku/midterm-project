Welcome to the World of Matcha

What is Matcha?

Matcha is a finely ground powder made from specially grown and processed green tea leaves. Unlike regular green tea, where leaves are steeped and discarded, matcha involves consuming the entire leaf in powdered form, which maximizes its nutritional value. Traditionally used in Japanese tea ceremonies, matcha is celebrated for its brilliant green color, slightly bitter yet umami-rich flavor, and numerous health benefits—including high levels of antioxidants, L-theanine for calm focus, and natural caffeine for gentle energy.

The Art of Japanese Tea Ceremony

Known as Sadō or Chadō, the Japanese tea ceremony is more than just drinking tea. It is a spiritual and cultural ritual that reflects the principles of harmony, respect, purity, and tranquility. Every movement carries symbolic meaning. The setting is designed to create a moment of mindfulness and connection between host and guest.

The Philosophy of Sadō

The tea ceremony embodies the spirit of wabi-sabi—finding beauty in imperfection and simplicity.

  • Wabi: Simple, rustic beauty
  • Sabi: The charm of timeworn objects

It also reflects omotenashi, or respectful hospitality, where the host creates a serene, welcoming atmosphere for guests.

History of Matcha

Who started Matcha in Japan?

The origins of matcha can be traced back to Tang Dynasty China. Zen Buddhist monks brought this method to Japan in the 12th century. Over time, matcha became deeply integrated into Japanese culture. In the 16th century, tea master Sen no Rikyū redefined the ceremony by emphasizing simplicity, humility, and spiritual awareness.

Japanese Spirits in Sado

Harmony and respect are core values in the Japanese tea ceremony.

The ritual fosters mindfulness, appreciation, and seasonal awareness.

Sado is not just about tea—it's a way of life and cultural expression.

Matcha is Worldwide

A modern global trend rooted in tradition

In recent years, matcha has gained global popularity not just as a traditional tea but as a modern lifestyle symbol. Cafés around the world serve matcha lattes, desserts, and more. Influencers and wellness advocates praise matcha for its clean energy boost and health benefits. Matcha has successfully blended ancient wisdom with modern wellness.
